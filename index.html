Key Design Decisions

Decision 01

MDM as External Golden Record

MDM holds Machine / Customer / Dealer / Part master. Foundry consumes via daily Magritte sync. MDM keys are canonical Ontology IDs. Telemetry events with unresolved serial numbers are quarantined.

Decision 02

Ontology-First Design, Incremental Population

Full Ontology (11 objects) designed on paper in Year 1. Populated incrementally: Machine + Alert (Year 1), + Component/Part/WorkOrder (Year 2), all objects (Year 3). Consumers build against the contract from Day 1.

Decision 03

Event-Sourced Path for Hot Domains

Engine and DPF/DEF bypass the hourly Gold batch. Kafka → Foundry AIP Streaming → Ontology direct write at ~30 sec latency. Batch Gold retained for analytics and ML training.

Decision 04

Machine Domain APIs as Abstraction Layer

Consuming apps (Member Portal, NBO/NBA Engine) never query the Foundry Ontology directly. Machine Domain APIs decouple app teams from Foundry internals and enable independent versioning.

Decision 05

NBO Urgency Score Formula

Score = (wear% × 0.35) + (fault_severity × 0.30) + (hours_remaining × 0.20) + (health_inverse × 0.10) + (historical_adj × 0.05). Levels: Critical ≥85, High 60–84, Medium 35–59, Routine <35.

Decision 06

ML Models Deferred to Year 3

Failure prediction and NBO propensity models require 12–18 months of clean Gold history. Building ML on Year 1 data produces noisy recommendations and loses stakeholder trust. Rule-based NBO in Year 2 buys time.
