MDM Integration Pattern

MDM is an external system of record. Foundry consumes golden records — it does not own them.

Design Rule: MDM keys are the canonical identifiers inside Foundry Ontology. When a Kafka telemetry event arrives with a machine serial number, Foundry resolves it against the MDM-sourced machine master in Bronze. If the serial number does not exist in MDM, the event is routed to a quarantine dataset — not ingested into Bronze.

Phased Ontology Build — Approach 3 (Incremental) + Approach 4 (Event-Sourced)

Design the full Ontology on paper in Year 1. Populate it incrementally. Migrate hot domains to event-sourced in Year 3.

Key Principle: Consuming teams (Member Portal, NBO/NBA Engine) build against the Ontology interface contract from Day 1 — even when objects are sparsely populated. The contract guarantees field names, types, and link shapes. Data density increases each phase. Applications do not need to change when new properties are added.

Architectural risk MDM serial # coverage gaps causing quarantine spikes. Kafka schema evolution breaking Magritte connectors. Gold layer trust — if Silver quality is poor, NBO recommendations are noisy. Schema contract discipline critical. Event-sourced Ontology ordering and exactly-once semantics. ML model drift without monitoring.
What you intentionally skip and why:
Year 1 skips Gold/Ontology — need validated Silver before building analytics on top. Building Gold on unvalidated data loses stakeholder trust fast.
Year 2 skips ML models — need 12–18 months of clean Gold history before training reliable failure prediction. Premature models produce noisy recommendations.
Year 3 event-sourced path is selective — only Engine and DPF where sub-minute alerting has a direct operational ROI. Running event-sourced for all 12 domains adds complexity without proportional value.
